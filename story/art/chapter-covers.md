# Chapter Covers — Image Prompts

*One wide CHAPTER-COVER image per unit (Prologue + Chapters 1–15) for* **The Accrual
World: How One Transaction Grew Up** *(working title). These are shown inside the app
at the head of each chapter to bring the book to life.*

**How to use this file**
- Each entry has a **chapter line** (number + title), a **scene note** (the dramatic
  beat the cover should capture), and an **IMAGE PROMPT** (subject and scene only).
- A shared **STYLE SUFFIX** is defined once below and is appended to every prompt at
  generation time — do **not** bake style words into the individual prompts.
- All covers are **wide, 16:9** (cover/banner aspect).
- Color world (use consistently across all covers): warm cream background **#fbf6ee**,
  dusty terracotta accent **#b5635a**, warm gold **#a88a5c**, dark-plum ink **#2a1f2d**.

**Cast reference (for visual consistency across covers):**
- **Sal** — a newborn transaction; render as a small, glowing, big-hearted coin-spirit
  / paper-and-light figure carrying a softly glowing "$90,000" mark. Eager, round-eyed.
- **Lyle** (the License, $72,000) — Sal's confident "instant" half; bright, quick,
  show-off; carries a glowing download key. **Sunny** (the Support, $18,000) — Sal's
  patient half; warmer, softer glow, chained at first inside a deferred-revenue dungeon.
- **The Friar** — Luca Pacioli, an ancient warm-eyed friar-mathematician in a monk's
  robe, Gandalf-by-way-of-a-monastery; carries a glowing ledger-staff inscribed
  A = L + E.
- **Remy** — anxious, earnest first-week junior accountant (the human avatar), satchel
  and notebook. **Iris Quinn** — sharp, kind controller in tailored clothes, Socratic,
  always one step ahead.
- **Tess & Hari** — Summit's two founders (Equity); hopeful, signing papers.
- **Tri** — a three-faced/three-armed bouncer-guardian at a gate, holding three
  documents. **The 606 Sphinx ("the Beast")** — a riddling sphinx with five eyes/five
  riddle-tablets. **The Allocator** — a fate-like, faceless figure with scales that
  splits one soul into two. **Lefty & Righty** — twin scribes, Debit (left) and Credit
  (right), bickering but inseparable. **Tally** — a proud, well-meaning balance-scale
  citizen (an honest liar). **Father Time** — the slow curse of aging/depreciation.
  **Rocky** — a sturdy, loyal server-rack/equipment creature that visibly ages.
  **The Oracle of Unpaid Debts** — a grim, fair seer in a cave. **The Ferryman of the
  Summary** — a Charon figure ferrying glowing temporary-souls across dark water.
  **The Auditor** — a stern, incorruptible inquisitor with a lantern and magnifying
  glass. **The Flattering Mirror** — a vain, ornate magic mirror showing a prettier
  reflection. **Atlas Corp** — the big reliable customer. **Pinnacle Inc.** — the
  customer who buys on credit then ghosts (bad debt). **Ranger Coffee Co.** — a cozy
  coffee-company creature Summit acquires.

## PROLOGUE — Welcome to the Accrual World
**Scene note:** A bird's-eye aerial tour of the whole realm — the one-way road of
accounting laid out from the Genesis gate to the distant Hall of Records, promising the
journey ahead.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A sweeping high aerial fantasy-map view of an entire whimsical realm called the Accrual
World, seen at golden hour. A single luminous one-way road winds from the foreground —
a Genesis loading-dock gate carved with the motto "everybody counts" — across the land
and through a chain of distinct fantastical stations: a war-room fortress, a triple
gate, a glowing labyrinth, a warehouse, a forge, a great domed library, a workshop, an
aging tower, three giant canvas-statements, a reading hall, a vaulted bank, a dark
river with a ferry, a crucible furnace, and finally a grand public stage / hall of
records glowing on the horizon. Tiny glowing coin-spirit transactions travel the road.
In the warm sky, a faint translucent equation A = L + E hovers like a constellation.
The whole scene feels like the opening map of a grand, friendly adventure.

## CHAPTER 1 — The Genesis & the Law of Physics
**Scene note:** Tess & Hari sign Summit into existence; $500,000 of cash thuds onto the
loading dock; the Friar states the Law and the whole world physically tilts until
equity rises to match.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
The Genesis station — a grand loading-dock and signing room at dawn. Two hopeful
founders, Tess and Hari, stand at a heavy oak desk having just signed a glowing charter,
while an enormous luminous pallet stamped "$500,000" of cash thuds onto the dock beside
them. The ancient warm-eyed Friar (a monk-mathematician with a glowing ledger-staff
inscribed A = L + E) raises a hand, and the entire architecture of the room visibly
TILTS like a vast balance scale — one side weighed down by the cash, the other rising
with a glowing column of "Equity" until the world levels out. A tiny newborn
coin-spirit, Sal, is faintly conceived as a shimmer of future possibility in the air.
Sense of cosmic law and new beginning.

## CHAPTER 2 — The War Room (Behind the Price Tag)
**Scene note:** Summit's leadership in the War Room decides to chase the Atlas deal and
forges the $90,000 price tag — the prequel to Sal's birth, full of cost charts and
break-even calculations.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A dramatic strategy War Room before any sale exists — a candlelit map-table command
center where Summit's leaders plan. The huge central table is a battle map not of
armies but of commerce: stacks of carabiners and climbing gear as game pieces,
rising-and-falling cost curves drawn in glowing chalk, a break-even line where two
plotted curves cross, and variance arrows. At the center, a price tag is being forged
on a small anvil and branded with a glowing "$90,000," sparks flying. Charts of fixed
vs variable costs hang like war banners on the walls. The mood is focused, secretive,
and clever — decisions being made that the outside world will never see.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A massive fortified gate guarded by Tri, a friendly-but-stern three-faced, three-armed
bouncer-guardian, each hand holding one glowing document: a Purchase Order, a Receiving
Report, and a Supplier Invoice. Before the gate stands Rocky — a sturdy, loyal

through. Two of Tri's documents glow matching green while the third flashes red
("invoice says 1,000, received 800"), and a heavy portcullis slams down as a "hard

checkpoint thriller, tense but fair — the loyal guardian against fraud.

## CHAPTER 4 — The Labyrinth of Revenue (and the Three States of Matter)
**Scene note:** The big one. Sal is born whole and proud at the Atlas signing, then in
the Labyrinth the Allocator splits Sal's soul into Lyle ($72,000) and Sunny ($18,000) —
Lyle struts off recognized instantly; Sunny is chained into the Deferred Revenue
dungeon.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
The emotional centerpiece — inside a glowing five-passage Labyrinth of Revenue guarded
by a riddling five-eyed Sphinx ("the Beast") perched above the entrance. At the heart of
the maze stands the Allocator: a tall, faceless, fate-like figure holding luminous
scales, in the very act of splitting one bright newborn coin-spirit, Sal (marked
"$90,000"), into two separate glowing figures. To the right, Lyle ($72,000) steps free
and confident into a beam of light, holding a glowing download key, already Somebody. To
the left, Sunny ($18,000) is pulled the other way, softer and sadder, chained at the
threshold of a shadowed "Deferred Revenue" dungeon she must earn her way out of. Three
symbolic doorways — liability, contract-asset, receivable — glow faintly around them.
A wound-of-the-heart moment: one hero cut in two.

## CHAPTER 5 — The Hall of Inventory (FIFO vs LIFO)
**Scene note:** Gear units line up on a cost-flow conveyor; when one sells, which cost
leaves with it? Two lines — FIFO (oldest cost exits first) vs LIFO (newest cost exits
first) — tell the same shelf two different stories.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A vast warehouse — the Hall of Inventory — dominated by a great mechanical cost-flow
conveyor belt. Identical stacks of climbing gear (carabiners, ropes) sit in three
labeled lots glowing with different price-tags ($10, $12, $14). The conveyor forks into
two parallel chutes that tell opposite stories: one chute labeled FIFO pulls the
oldest, cheapest box off first; the other labeled LIFO pulls the newest, priciest box
off first — the same physical shelf, two different costs leaving by different doors. A
small whimsical side-vignette contrasts a carton of milk (perishable, FIFO) with a
stack of steel bars (LIFO). Gauges showing COGS, net income, and tax tick to different
values on each chute. Clever, mechanical, "same facts, two truths."

## CHAPTER 6 — The Matching Forge (ASC 340-40)
**Scene note:** The $15,000 sales commission that landed Sal must not be expensed all at
once — at the Forge it is welded into a Deferred Contract Cost asset and chipped away
$416.67 a month, perfectly in step with Sunny's support over three years.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A warm, glowing blacksmith's forge — the Matching Forge — where costs are welded to the
revenues they earn. A master smith hammers a bright bar branded "$15,000 Commission" on
the anvil, but instead of burning it up all at once, sparks of it are being carefully
welded onto a long glowing timeline rail that stretches into the distance, divided into
36 evenly spaced monthly segments. Tiny matched pairs of light — a cost-spark linked to
a revenue-spark — travel the rail together. In the background, faintly, Sunny's softly
glowing figure walks the same 36-month path in step with the cost. A prepaid-insurance
jar glows on a side shelf as a smaller example. Mood: craftsmanship, patience, "match
the cost to the revenue."

## CHAPTER 7 — The Great Library (Journalizing, Ledgers & the Trial Balance That Lies)
**Scene note:** Every entry so far arrives to be written down; twin scribes Lefty
(Debit) and Righty (Credit) bicker over who matters more while Tally the Trial Balance
proudly declares "it balances!" — even though balanced is not the same as true.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A magnificent domed Great Library of accounts, shelves spiraling up into golden light.
At a grand double desk sit twin scribe-spirits: Lefty (Debit) on the left and Righty
(Credit) on the right, quills in hand, mid-bicker over a shared giant open journal, each
gesturing that their own side matters more — yet a glowing thread binds them equal. A
huge "Master Sorting Hat" / general-ledger mechanism sorts glowing entries into
neighborhood pigeonholes (subsidiary ledgers for Atlas, Pinnacle, and others). In the
foreground stands Tally — a proud, well-meaning balance-scale citizen — holding up a
perfectly level scale and beaming "it balances!", while one tiny entry glows in the
wrong drawer behind it, unnoticed. The Friar watches knowingly from a gallery. Warm,
scholarly, a touch of comic pride.

## CHAPTER 8 — The Workshop of Adjustments
**Scene note:** The Workshop fixes the world Tally left "balanced but wrong" — a great
2×2 grid on the wall sorts every adjustment into four cells (deferred revenue, prepaid
expense, accrued revenue, accrued expense) while a bank reconciliation reconciles two
disagreeing ledgers.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A cozy, busy clockwork Workshop of Adjustments, its back wall dominated by a giant
glowing 2×2 grid — four labeled cells (deferred revenue, prepaid expense, accrued
revenue, accrued expense) — like a master key, with little mechanisms feeding each cell.
At workbenches, Remy-like clerk-spirits fine-tune the world: a prepaid-insurance jar
slowly draining into an expense flame, Sunny's support dripping coin by coin from a
"liability" tank into a "revenue" basin, and a pile of unpaid wages stacking up as an
accrued expense. On one bench, two ledgers — "Book" and "Bank" — are being reconciled
with bridging threads (outstanding checks, deposits in transit). Gears, gauges, and soft
lamplight. Mood: tinkering the numbers from "balanced" into "true."

**Scene note:** Father Time visits Rocky and the loyal equipment creature ages year by
year (depreciation), while across the realm the grim Oracle foresees that Pinnacle's

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A split-mood chamber. On the left, the Aging Room: the towering, hourglass-bodied figure

glowing pool and foresees the doom of a receivable — the customer Pinnacle Inc. fading
to a ghost — while pointing to an "allowance" jar that must be filled with set-aside
coins now, before the loss arrives. Themes of time, foresight, and prudent grief.

## CHAPTER 10 — The Trinity Hall (The Financial Statements)
**Scene note:** Sal, now scattered across many accounts, sees themselves assembled into
a whole picture for the first time — three great connected canvases (income statement,
balance sheet, cash flow) that articulate and tie out. The emotional high.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A soaring cathedral-like Trinity Hall holding three enormous illuminated canvases side
by side, joined by glowing connective threads: the first canvas is "the movie of a
period" (an income statement flowing revenue down to net income), the second is "a
snapshot in time" (a balance sheet visibly balancing assets against liabilities plus
equity), the third is a "lie-detector" cash-flow canvas bridging net income to cash.
Bright threads of light visibly link them — net income flowing into retained earnings,
ending cash tying across — so they articulate as one. In the center, the small radiant
coin-spirit Sal, once scattered, stands gazing up at the three canvases and seeing their
whole self reflected for the very first time, awestruck. Triumphant, luminous, the
emotional summit of the journey.

## CHAPTER 11 — The Reading Room (Ratios, Frameworks & the Fine Print)
**Scene note:** Outsider analysts arrive to read Summit's story — turning numbers into
sentences via ratios and DuPont, comparing two rulebooks (GAAP vs IFRS), and watching
leases climb onto the balance sheet.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
An elegant, hushed Reading Room — part grand reading library, part observatory — where
outside analysts come to interpret Summit's published story. Scholarly reader-figures
study the three glowing statements through magnifying lenses, deriving glowing
ratio-sentences (liquidity, solvency, profitability, efficiency) that float up as
constellations. A central DuPont apparatus decomposes a glowing "ROE" into three turning
gears: margin × turnover × leverage. To one side, two great rulebooks face each other on
matching lecterns — "GAAP" and "IFRS" — like two dialects of the same language (one
crossing out LIFO). In a corner, a lease that once floated off to the side visibly
climbs up and settles onto the balance sheet as a right-of-use asset. Intellectual,
refined, "numbers are nouns; ratios are sentences."

## CHAPTER 12 — The Vault of Mergers
**Scene note:** Summit acquires Ranger Coffee Co.; the premium paid that can't be pinned
to any identifiable asset becomes Goodwill ("the price of believing"), two companies
consolidate into one set of books, and intercompany sales are eliminated.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A grand domed Vault of Mergers, like a bank treasury crossed with a wedding hall. Two
company-creatures come together: Summit (the established outdoor-tech firm) formally
acquiring the cozy, warm Ranger Coffee Co. creature (steam curling from its coffee-pot
form). Their separate piles of assets are being fair-valued on glowing scales and then
merged into a single consolidated ledger-book; bridging threads marked "intercompany"
are being snipped and eliminated. Rising above the merged pile, a shimmering golden orb
labeled "Goodwill" floats — the premium paid that pins to no physical asset, "the price
of believing." In a shadowed alcove, a smaller cracked "impairment" warning hints the
orb could be carved down if the dream sours. Ceremonial, golden, a little bittersweet.

## CHAPTER 13 — The Great Cleansing (Closing & Reversing Entries)
**Scene note:** The emotional climax. At year-end the Ferryman of the Summary comes for
the temporary citizens — including Sal — and carries Sal's net contribution across dark
water to the permanent shore of Retained Earnings: not death, but rebirth.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A solemn, beautiful night scene at the year-end shore — the Great Cleansing. The
Ferryman of the Summary, a gentle Charon-like robed figure poling a glowing barge,
carries luminous temporary-souls (revenues and expenses) across a wide, dark, starlit
river. At the bow stands Sal, the small radiant coin-spirit hero, looking back one last
time with bittersweet acceptance — fading as a "temporary" yet glowing brighter as their
essence streams forward. On the far shore rises a warm, eternal lighthouse-monument
labeled "Retained Earnings," its light absorbing the arriving souls into permanent
golden equity — death turning to rebirth. Behind, the old temporary accounts dim to
zero, a fresh year waiting. Reverent, cinematic, the emotional peak of the whole book.

## CHAPTER 14 — The Crucible (The Independent Audit)
**Scene note:** Before Summit can go public, the stern incorruptible Auditor puts the
books in the Crucible — literally walking Sal's $90,000 back to the signed contract and
download-key handover, predicting payroll without a single timesheet, and challenging
the soft estimates.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A dramatic forge-like chamber called the Crucible, lit by a great furnace, where the
books are tested by fire. The Auditor — a stern, incorruptible inquisitor in a long coat
with a bright lantern and an oversized magnifying glass — holds up the glowing
"$90,000" Sal-number and traces a luminous evidence-thread backward through the air to a
signed contract and a download-key handover ("prove control transferred"). Pinned to one
wall, an independent analytical prediction of payroll glows beside the actual figure,
matching exactly without a single timesheet consulted. Nearby, soft glowing estimate-orbs
(the allowance, the goodwill impairment) are held up to the furnace-light and scrutinized
for reasonableness; a tiny found error is being circled and corrected. Mood: rigorous,
incorruptible, "trust is nice; evidence is better."

## CHAPTER 15 — The Public Stage (The 10-K, the Earnings Call & Recognition at Last)
**Scene note:** The finale and epilogue. The 10-K is filed and Sal's number is printed
in the Hall of Records — recognition at last; on the earnings-call stage the Flattering
Mirror shows Adjusted EBITDA while the Referee forces the true GAAP reflection beside it;
then, at midnight, a new transaction is born and the cycle begins again.

**IMAGE PROMPT:**
A grand triumphant Public Stage under bright lights, fused with a vast Hall of Records.
Center stage, an enormous open public ledger-tome — the 10-K — has Sal's glowing number
permanently inscribed onto its page, radiant and finally seen; Sal, the coin-spirit hero,
stands small and proud in the spotlight, recognized at last and remembered. To one side,
executives present at an earnings-call podium beside an ornate, vain Flattering Mirror
that shows a prettier "Adjusted EBITDA" reflection, while a striped Referee figure firmly
holds up the true "GAAP" reflection right beside it, at least as prominent. Far in the
background, through a doorway, dawn breaks on a new fiscal year: a brand-new tiny
coin-spirit transaction is being born on the Genesis loading dock as the Friar smiles —
the cycle beginning again. Celebratory, golden, full-circle, a little wink to the future.
